## Lakeside Magic in Coeur d'Alene
Idaho's gem—lakefront dining beats any drive-thru. Reviews love the ambiance, like Season's happy hour. Reddit must-eats include Syringa sushi, but lakeside wins for scenery. Spots date back, surviving tourism booms with fresh catches. The lake's right there, so you get fish practically jumping onto your plate (okay, not literally). X users rave about the vibe—think sunset dinners that make you forget you're just passing through. These spots use local ingredients, like Idaho trout or huckleberries, tying the meal to the land. It's the kind of stop that makes I-90 feel like a destination, not a slog.

## Top Lakeside Dining Spots
1. **Cedars Floating Restaurant** - Literally on water. Famous: Seafood specials pre-5 PM—salmon or prawns, buttery and fresh. Reddit: "Saves cash, great drinks." Since 1960s, iconic float. I-90 direct via US-95. Price: $25-35. Humor: Views so good, you'll forget the bill. Try their clam chowder; it's a local legend.

2. **Beverly's at CDA Resort** - Upscale views. Iconic: Oysters—fresh, briny. X: "Gorgeous, must-play." Resort staple since '80s. Price: $30-40. Dry wit: Fancy, but your jeans are probably fine.

3. **Trails End Brewery** - Casual lakeside. Famous: Egg Benedict Pizza—brunch twist. X/YouTube: "America's Best." Newer, but views kill. Near lake off I-90. Price: $15-25. Chuckle: Beer and pizza? Genius move.

4. **Season Restaurant** - Happy hour king. Seafood focus—halibut tacos are a hit. Reddit: "Best before 5." Waterfront since 2010s. Price: $20-30. Fun fact: Cocktails steal the show.

5. **Tony's on the Lake** - Cozy Italian. Pasta with views—carbonara famed for creamy richness. Reviews: "Authentic." Short from I-90. Price: $18-28. Wrap-up: Lake vibes elevate everything.
